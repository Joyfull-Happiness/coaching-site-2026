And I do all of it by building microhabit systems that work with my brain instead of against it. People ask me how. That's exactly what I help others unlock.

I was the person who started everything. Here's how I learned to finish.

I'm not going to pretend I picked one lane and stayed in it. I coach ADHD professionals. I'm the NS Strategies Coach at an AI school. I host a podcast. I write music. I talk to my plants. And somehow — all of it works.

But it didn't always. I used to be the person who had a hundred ideas, a dozen half-finished plans, and zero follow-through. Every productivity system I tried worked for everyone else. I'd build an elaborate plan, get interrupted by life, and call the unfinished work failure. Then I'd start over. Again.

The problem was never my brain. It was every system demanding my brain be something it's not.

Here's what nobody tells you: the problem isn't your discipline, your focus, or your willpower. It's that you've been trying to run ADHD software on systems built for a different brain. Perfectionism designing every task too big to survive your real, unpredictable, neurodivergent life — that's not a character flaw. That's a systems problem.

The moment I stopped blaming my brain and started building systems around how it actually works — everything changed. Not because life got simpler. Because I stopped waiting for perfect conditions and started building for the imperfect ones.

Now I help others do the same. I work with ADHD professionals in tech and beyond — smart, capable people full of ideas who can't stay consistent. Not because they're broken — because every system they've tried was built for someone else's brain. I build systems for their brain instead.

I love what I do. I love that I get to have all of these interests and thrive in all of them. And I love showing other people that they can too — once they stop fighting their brain and start working with it.

My Certifications & What They Mean For You

These aren't just letters after my name. Each certification shapes how I coach — and directly impacts the results you get.

ACC

Associate Certified Coach — International Coaching Federation

The ICF is the gold standard in professional coaching worldwide, with over 50,000 members across 150+ countries. My ACC credential means I've completed rigorous coach-specific training, logged 100+ hours of real coaching experience, and passed a comprehensive knowledge assessment. For you, it means every session follows evidence-based coaching competencies — active listening, powerful questioning, and facilitating growth — not just advice-giving. ICF credentialing also requires ongoing ethics training and continuing education, so my practice stays current and accountable.

iNLP

Certified NLP Practitioner — iNLP Center

Neuro-Linguistic Programming is the study of how language and mental patterns shape your behavior, habits, and emotional responses. NLP is the same methodology behind Tony Robbins' entire coaching career — he trained directly under NLP co-founder John Grinder, and it's the foundation of everything he teaches. Oprah Winfrey has also credited NLP techniques in her work. My iNLP certification means I'm trained to identify and rewire the unconscious patterns that keep you stuck — like the perfectionism loop that inflates your tasks, or the self-talk that convinces you "good enough" isn't enough. It's the reason my coaching changes how you think, not just what you do.

What I Believe

01

Consistency Over Correctness

Reps over perfection. The ADHD brain doesn't need a perfect plan — it needs a microhabit small enough to do even on the hard days. That's where momentum lives.

02

Micro-Actions Over Master Plans

If a task can't survive a real, unpredictable, neurodivergent day with interruptions, it's too big. Shrink the action, protect the streak, let the habit do the heavy lifting.

03

Mastery Through Experimentation

Mastery doesn't come from getting it right. It comes from experimentation — running tests on your own brain, learning what fuels you, and building from there. Perfection is a trap. Reps are the game.
