In the event of any conflict between this Policy and the Terms and Conditions, the Terms and Conditions shall govern unless the conflict relates specifically to data privacy rights afforded to you by applicable law, in which case the provision offering greater protection shall prevail.

2. Information We Collect

We collect information in the following categories and through the following means:

2.1 Information You Provide Directly. We collect information that you voluntarily submit to us, including but not limited to:

(a) Contact and Identity Information: Full name, email address, phone number, mailing address, date of birth, and any other identifying information you provide when booking a session, creating an account, or contacting us.

(b) Payment and Billing Information: Credit card numbers, debit card numbers, bank account information, billing address, and other financial data necessary to process your payments. Please note that payment processing is handled by third-party payment processors, and we do not store your full credit card or bank account numbers on our servers. We may retain partial payment information (such as the last four digits of your card number and expiration date) for record-keeping and dispute resolution purposes.

(c) Coaching-Related Information: Goals, challenges, personal history, professional background, health-related information (only to the extent voluntarily shared by you in the coaching context), relationship information, financial circumstances, career details, and any other personal information you share during intake forms, assessments, questionnaires, coaching sessions, or communications with us.

(d) Communications: The content of emails, text messages, chat messages, voicemails, direct messages, and any other communications you send to us or that occur during the coaching engagement.

(e) Feedback and Testimonials: Reviews, testimonials, survey responses, feedback forms, and any other evaluative content you provide.

(f) Account Information: Username, password, account preferences, and profile information if you create an account on our platforms.

2.2 Information Collected Automatically. When you visit our website or interact with our digital platforms, we may automatically collect certain information, including but not limited to:

(a) Device and Browser Information: IP address, browser type and version, operating system, device type, device identifiers, screen resolution, and language preferences.

(b) Usage Data: Pages viewed, links clicked, time spent on pages, referring and exit URLs, date and time of access, and navigation paths through our website.

(c) Location Data: General geographic location as inferred from your IP address. We do not collect precise geolocation data unless you explicitly consent.

(d) Cookies and Tracking Technologies: We use cookies, pixel tags, web beacons, local storage, and similar technologies to collect information about your browsing behavior and preferences. For more information, see Section 8 (Cookies and Tracking Technologies) of this Policy.

2.3 Information from Third-Party Sources. We may receive information about you from third-party sources, including but not limited to:

(a) Payment Processors: Transaction confirmations, payment status, and limited billing information from services such as Stripe, PayPal, Square, or other processors we use.

(b) Booking and Scheduling Platforms: Appointment details, scheduling preferences, and contact information from platforms such as Calendly, Acuity, or similar tools.

(c) Social Media Platforms: Publicly available profile information, engagement data, and content you share when you interact with our social media accounts or use social media features on our website.

(d) Marketing and Analytics Providers: Demographic data, interest-based data, and behavioral data from advertising platforms, analytics services, and marketing tools.

(e) Referrals: Contact information and referral context provided by individuals who refer you to our Services.

3.6 Legal Compliance and Protection. To comply with applicable laws, regulations, and legal obligations; respond to subpoenas, court orders, or other legal processes; enforce our Terms and Conditions and other agreements; protect our rights, property, and safety; and prevent, detect, or investigate fraud, security issues, or other potentially prohibited or unlawful activities.

3.7 Business Operations. To manage our internal business operations, including record-keeping, accounting, auditing, data analysis, and internal reporting.

3.8 Research and Development. To conduct research, analysis, and development aimed at improving our coaching methodologies, creating new programs, and enhancing the quality and effectiveness of our Services. Any information used for these purposes will be anonymized and de-identified so that it cannot reasonably be linked back to you.

4.1 Contractual Necessity. Processing is necessary for the performance of our coaching agreement with you, including delivering Coaching Services, processing payments, and communicating about your sessions.

4.2 Legitimate Interests. Processing is necessary for our legitimate business interests, such as improving our Services, marketing our offerings, protecting our legal rights, and managing our business operations, provided that such interests are not overridden by your data protection rights.

4.3 Consent. Where required by law, we process your personal data based on your explicit consent, such as for sending marketing communications or using cookies and tracking technologies. You may withdraw your consent at any time without affecting the lawfulness of processing based on consent before its withdrawal.

4.4 Legal Obligation. Processing is necessary to comply with legal obligations to which we are subject, such as tax reporting, record-keeping requirements, and responding to lawful requests from public authorities.

6. Data Retention

6.1 Retention Period. We retain your personal information for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected, including to satisfy legal, accounting, reporting, or regulatory requirements. The specific retention period depends on the nature of the information and the purpose for which it was collected, as follows:

(a) Coaching Records: Session notes, intake forms, assessments, and coaching-related information are retained for a period of seven (7) years after the conclusion of the coaching engagement, unless a longer retention period is required by law.

(b) Financial and Transaction Records: Payment records, invoices, and billing information are retained for a period of seven (7) years in accordance with IRS record-keeping requirements and applicable Washington State tax laws.

(c) Marketing and Communication Records: Email addresses and communication preferences are retained until you opt out of marketing communications or request deletion, subject to our legal record-keeping obligations.

(d) Website and Analytics Data: Automatically collected data (cookies, usage data, device information) is retained for up to twenty-four (24) months, unless a shorter period is required by applicable law.

6.2 Deletion. Upon expiration of the applicable retention period, or upon your valid request for deletion (subject to Section 7), we will securely dispose of or anonymize your personal information so that it can no longer be associated with you.

7. Your Rights and Choices

7.2 Right to Correction. You have the right to request that we correct any inaccurate or incomplete personal information we hold about you.

7.3 Right to Deletion. You have the right to request that we delete your personal information, subject to certain exceptions (such as where retention is required by law or necessary to complete a transaction you initiated).

7.4 Right to Restrict Processing. In certain circumstances, you have the right to request that we restrict the processing of your personal information.

7.5 Right to Data Portability. Where applicable, you have the right to receive a copy of your personal information in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format and to transmit that information to another controller.

7.6 Right to Object. You have the right to object to the processing of your personal information for certain purposes, including direct marketing.

7.7 Right to Withdraw Consent. Where processing is based on your consent, you have the right to withdraw your consent at any time without affecting the lawfulness of processing that occurred before withdrawal.

7.9 Washington State Privacy Rights. If you are a Washington State resident, you may have additional rights under the Washington Privacy Act (WPA) and the Washington My Health My Data Act (MHMDA), including:

(a) The right to know whether we are collecting, sharing, or selling your personal data, including health-related data;

(b) The right to access your personal data;

(c) The right to delete your personal data;

(d) The right to correct inaccurate personal data;

(e) The right to opt out of the processing of your personal data for purposes of targeted advertising, the sale of personal data, or profiling in furtherance of decisions that produce legal or similarly significant effects;

(f) The right to obtain your consent before we collect or share your health data (as defined under MHMDA), and the right to revoke such consent at any time; and

(g) The right to appeal our decision regarding a privacy rights request.

7.10 California Privacy Rights. If you are a California resident, you may have additional rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA), including the right to know, delete, correct, and opt out of the sale or sharing of your personal information. To the extent these laws apply, we will honor your rights as required.

7.11 Exercising Your Rights. To exercise any of the rights described above, please contact us using the information provided in Section 13. We will respond to verifiable requests within the timeframes required by applicable law (typically within 45 days for U.S. state privacy laws and 30 days for GDPR). 7.12 Appeals. If you are dissatisfied with our response to a privacy rights request, you may appeal by contacting us at the information provided in Section 13. If your appeal is denied, you may contact your state's Attorney General or the relevant data protection authority.

8.1 Types of Cookies We Use. We use the following categories of cookies and tracking technologies:

(a) Strictly Necessary Cookies: These cookies are essential for the basic functionality of our website and cannot be disabled. They enable core features such as security, account access, and payment processing.

(b) Performance and Analytics Cookies: These cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting and reporting information anonymously. We use services such as Google Analytics to analyze website traffic and usage patterns.

(c) Functional Cookies: These cookies enable enhanced functionality and personalization, such as remembering your preferences, language settings, and login information.

(d) Marketing and Advertising Cookies: These cookies are used to deliver relevant advertisements and track the effectiveness of our marketing campaigns. They may be set by us or by third-party advertising partners and may track your browsing activity across different websites.

8.2 Third-Party Cookies. Some cookies on our website are placed by third-party service providers, including but not limited to Google Analytics, Meta (Facebook) Pixel, and other advertising and analytics platforms. These third parties may collect information about your online activities over time and across different websites. We do not control the cookies or tracking technologies used by third parties and recommend reviewing their respective privacy policies.

8.3 Managing Cookies. You can control and manage cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to block or delete cookies, set preferences for certain websites, and browse in private or incognito mode. Please note that disabling certain cookies may affect the functionality of our website. You may also opt out of certain analytics and advertising cookies by visiting the Digital Advertising Alliance's opt-out page (optout.aboutads.info) or the Network Advertising Initiative's opt-out page (optout.networkadvertising.org).

8.4 Do Not Track. Some browsers transmit "Do Not Track" (DNT) signals. Our website does not currently respond to DNT signals. However, you can exercise your privacy rights as described in Section 7.

(c) Sharing and Selling: We do not sell consumer health data. We will not share consumer health data with third parties without your separate, express consent, except as necessary to provide the Services or as required by law.
